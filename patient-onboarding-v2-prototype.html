Two quick authorizations.

These let us legally retrieve your medical records and use them to refine your matches. You can withdraw consent anytime.

HIPAA Authorization

Authorizes Resonata to use your health information to find matching care options and contact you about them. Required for either path.

HIPAA Authorization for Use and Disclosure of Health Information

By signing this authorization, you allow Resonata to receive, use, and disclose your protected health information (PHI) for the specific purposes described below.

Information that may be used or disclosed: Information about your medical history, diagnoses, lab results, medications, treatments, imaging, and other health information needed to determine eligibility for clinical trials and care options.

Purposes: Matching you to clinical trials and treatment options; communicating with you about those options; sharing relevant subsets of your information with research sites or treating clinicians only when you choose to apply to a specific option.

Recipients: Resonata staff with a need to know; designated technology vendors operating under business associate agreements; sites or sponsors only when you initiate an application.

Expiration: This authorization expires three (3) years from the date of signature unless you revoke it earlier.

Right to revoke: You may revoke this authorization at any time in writing or through your account settings. Revocation does not affect uses already made before we received your revocation.

No conditioning: Resonata will not condition treatment, payment, enrollment, or eligibility for benefits on whether you sign this authorization.

Re-disclosure: Information disclosed under this authorization may be subject to re-disclosure by the recipient and may no longer be protected by federal privacy law.

Records Retrieval Authorization (DNAvisit)

DNAvisit uses Health Information Exchange data to securely retrieve your medical records from your providers. Usually takes about 8 hours.

Authorization for Records Retrieval through DNAvisit

By signing this authorization, you allow DNAvisit, on Resonata's behalf, to securely retrieve your medical records from your healthcare providers through Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) and other lawful sources.

What DNAvisit will retrieve: Medical records that are available through HIE networks your providers participate in, including diagnoses, lab results, imaging reports, medications, treatment history, and clinical notes.

How it works: DNAvisit submits a records request on your behalf using the identifying information you've provided. Records are encrypted in transit, processed within DNAvisit's secure environment, and delivered to Resonata for use under the HIPAA authorization you signed above.

Retention by DNAvisit: DNAvisit holds your records only as long as needed to fulfill the request and then deletes them, unless you've authorized longer retention.

Right to revoke: You may revoke this authorization at any time. Revocation stops future retrievals and triggers deletion of any DNAvisit-held copies. Records already delivered to Resonata are governed by the HIPAA authorization above.

Security posture: DNAvisit operates under a business associate agreement with Resonata and is HIPAA-compliant. Records are encrypted at rest and in transit. Access is logged and audited.

What this authorization does not do: This authorization does not give Resonata or DNAvisit permission to share your records with employers, insurance carriers, or anyone outside the matching purpose.

What this trial is studying

Nipocalimab is an investigational therapy that targets FcRn, which controls how long certain antibodies (including the ones causing MG) stay in your body. The trial is testing whether lowering those antibodies improves symptoms over 24 weeks.
