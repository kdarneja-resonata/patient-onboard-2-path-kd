💊 How Imaavy works

Imaavy (nipocalimab-aahu) is a high-affinity humanized monoclonal antibody that works by blocking the neonatal Fc receptor (FcRn). By binding FcRn, Imaavy reduces the recycling of IgG antibodies — including the harmful autoantibodies (anti-AChR and anti-MuSK) that attack the neuromuscular junction and cause myasthenia gravis symptoms.

It is FDA approved for adults and pediatric patients 12 years and older with generalized myasthenia gravis who are AChR or MuSK antibody positive.

📋 What to expect

  • Dosing: Loading dose of 30 mg/kg IV over 60 minutes on Day 1, then 15 mg/kg IV every 2 weeks starting at Week 2.
  • Setting: Administered by a healthcare provider as an intravenous (IV) infusion — typically in a clinic or infusion center.
  • Monitoring: IgG levels will be monitored periodically. Your doctor may adjust dosing based on your response.
  • Duration: Treatment continues as long as your doctor determines it's beneficial. Each infusion takes approximately 60 minutes.
  • Drug interactions: Tell your doctor about all medications, especially live or live-attenuated vaccines (should be up to date before starting).

⚠️ Safety profile

Most common adverse reactions (≥10% in clinical trials):

  • Headache
  • Upper respiratory infection
  • Nausea
  • Fatigue
  • Injection site reactions

Serious risks to discuss with your doctor:

  • Serious infections (Imaavy reduces IgG antibodies broadly — ensure vaccinations are current before starting)
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (including anaphylaxis)
  • Thromboembolic events

Do not use Imaavy if you:

  • Have a known serious hypersensitivity to nipocalimab-aahu or any of its excipients
  • Have an unresolved serious infection

This is not a complete list. See the Important Safety Information below and talk to your doctor about your specific situation.

Important Safety Information

Imaavy® (nipocalimab-aahu)

SERIOUS INFECTIONS
Imaavy reduces IgG antibody levels, which may increase the risk of serious infections. Ensure patients are current on all recommended vaccinations prior to initiating Imaavy. Monitor for signs and symptoms of infection during treatment. Withhold Imaavy for serious infections and resume when the infection has resolved.

HYPERSENSITIVITY REACTIONS
Serious hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis, have been observed with Imaavy. Discontinue the infusion immediately for severe hypersensitivity reactions and institute appropriate medical treatment. Imaavy is contraindicated in patients with known serious hypersensitivity to nipocalimab-aahu or any of its excipients.

THROMBOEMBOLIC EVENTS
Thromboembolic events have been reported with Imaavy and other FcRn inhibitors. Monitor patients for signs and symptoms of thromboembolic events and treat appropriately.

REDUCTION IN PROTECTIVE ANTIBODIES
Imaavy reduces total IgG levels, including protective antibodies against infections. This may increase susceptibility to infections. Consider the benefits and risks before initiating Imaavy in patients with a history of recurrent infections.

ADVERSE REACTIONS
The most common adverse reactions (incidence ≥10% and at least 2% greater than placebo) in the ADAPT-NXT trial were: headache, upper respiratory infection, nausea, fatigue, and injection site reactions.

USE IN SPECIFIC POPULATIONS
Pregnancy: May cause fetal harm based on its mechanism of action. Advise patients of the potential risk to the fetus and to use effective contraception.
Lactation: Advise patients that breastfeeding is not recommended during treatment with Imaavy.
