## Product Information

### Core Product

**Product Name:** Resonata Health Platform

**One-Line Description:**
Resonata matches patients to treatments, clinical trials, and diagnostics using their medical records, making biopharma outreach 10-20x more efficient than traditional methods.

**Detailed Description:**
Resonata Health is a precision patient-matching platform that connects patients to the right treatment options based on their actual medical history. Unlike broad, demographic-based marketing, Resonata uses clinical reasoning grounded in patient records to match people to approved treatments, clinical trials, and diagnostic tests they're genuinely eligible for.

The platform serves two sides of a marketplace: patients and providers on one side (discovering and acting on care options), and biopharma sponsors and clinical enrollers on the other (reaching qualified patients efficiently). This creates a network effect where more patients means more value for sponsors, and better-matched options means more value for patients.

The first commercial use case is treatment matching for an approved therapy, with clinical trial recruitment as a near-future priority.

### Product Suite

The platform has four apps owned across two product areas:

**Healthcare (Lauren's ownership):**
- **Patient App** - Patients sign up directly, see matched treatment/trial options based on their medical records
- **Provider App** - Clinical staff view and act on care options for their patients

**B2B (KD's ownership):**
- **Sponsor App (Workbench)** - Biopharma commercial teams launch targeted campaigns with medical eligibility criteria
- **Enroller App (Workbench)** - Clinical staff at trial sites receive qualified patient leads for enrollment

**Key Features:**
1. Precision matching based on patient medical records
2. Eligibility criteria filtering for treatments and trials
3. Direct patient sign-up (no provider referral required)
4. Sponsor campaign management with targeting criteria
5. Enroller workflow for clinical trial patient delivery

## Target Market

### Customer Segments

**Primary Customer (Payer):** Biopharma sponsors
- **Who:** Commercial teams at biopharma companies
- **Use case:** Reach patients eligible for their approved treatments or trials
- **Pain point:** $49B in inefficient marketing spend; traditional outreach is broad and wasteful
- **Pricing:** Per campaign OR per matched patient

**Secondary Customer:** Clinical trial enrollers
- **Who:** Clinical staff at research sites running trials
- **Use case:** Receive pre-qualified patient leads to hit enrollment targets faster
- **Pain point:** Trial recruitment is slow, expensive, and often fails to meet timelines

**End Users (non-paying but critical):**
- **Patients** - Sign up directly to discover matched care options
- **Healthcare providers** - View options for their patients

### Buyer Personas

**Persona 1: "The Sponsor" - Biopharma Commercial/Medical Affairs Team**
- **Role:** Launch and manage targeted patient awareness campaigns for approved therapies
- **Goals:** Reach the right patients efficiently; demonstrate ROI vs. traditional DTC marketing
- **Challenges:** Broad marketing wastes budget; hard to prove patient-level impact
- **Motivations:** Efficiency, compliance, proving the channel works for future budgets
- **Decision criteria:** Reach (how many eligible patients), conversion rate, cost per matched patient
- **Quote:** "We need to find patients who are actually eligible, not just people who saw a TV ad."

**Persona 2: "The Enroller" - Clinical Trial Site Coordinator**
- **Role:** Identify and qualify patients for clinical trials at their site
- **Goals:** Hit enrollment targets on time without overwhelming clinical staff
- **Challenges:** Traditional recruitment is slow; screening failures waste everyone's time
- **Motivations:** Trial success, efficiency, less manual outreach
- **Decision criteria:** Patient quality (pre-qualified), speed to first patient, ease of workflow
- **Quote:** "We spend so much time screening patients who aren't even close to eligible."

**Persona 3: "The Patient" - Individual seeking care options**
- **Role:** Find out what treatments or trials they may be eligible for
- **Goals:** Access to options they wouldn't find on their own; feel seen and matched
- **Challenges:** Hard to know what's out there; don't want to be marketed to generically
- **Motivations:** Better health outcomes, access to cutting-edge treatments
- **Decision criteria:** Trust, relevance of matches, ease of sign-up
- **Quote:** "I didn't even know there was a trial for my condition nearby."

## Value Proposition

### Problem Statement

**The Problem:**
Biopharma companies spend ~$49B annually on patient marketing that's largely inefficient. Traditional outreach is demographic-based, not clinically-based, meaning most people reached aren't actually eligible. At the same time, patients who could benefit from approved treatments or trials never find out they qualify. Clinical trials routinely miss enrollment targets because finding and screening eligible patients is slow and manual.

### Solution Statement

**Our Solution:**
Resonata uses patients' medical records to match them to treatments and trials they're actually eligible for. Sponsors get precise reach. Patients get relevant options. Enrollers get pre-qualified leads. Everyone gets more efficiency and better outcomes.

### Unique Value Proposition

**What makes us different:**
1. Clinical reasoning grounded in actual medical records (not demographics or self-reported data)
2. Two-sided marketplace: value compounds as patient network grows
3. Single platform spanning treatment awareness and clinical trial recruitment
